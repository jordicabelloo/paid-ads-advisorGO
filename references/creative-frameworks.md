# Creative Frameworks

## The Rule of One (Brandon Willington)

Every winning ad communicates:
- **ONE idea** — a single concept the viewer takes away
- **ONE story** — one narrative thread, not a montage
- **ONE promise** — one specific outcome
- **ONE emotion** — fear, hope, desire, frustration, relief

Testing priority: Idea > Story > Promise > Emotion.
The idea (angle) has the biggest impact on performance.

## Ad Structure: Hook → Retention → Bridge + CTA

### Hook (first 1.8 seconds)

The average attention span on social media is 1.8 seconds. If you don't hook them, nothing else matters.

**Hook types:**
- Bold claim: "I spent $20K yesterday on ads and made $60K"
- Question: "Why are 9 out of 10 coaches failing to get clients?"
- Pattern interrupt: Visual disruption, unexpected opening
- Empathy: "If you're tired of [exact frustration]..."
- Committed language filter: Include words that qualify the viewer

**Committed language examples:**
| Weak (curious) | Strong (committed) |
|----------------|-------------------|
| "How guys lose belly fat" | "How the guys I coach lose belly fat" |
| "Start a business" | "Start a business despite your 9-to-5" |
| "Get more clients" | "Get 10 high-ticket clients this month" |

### Retention (middle section)

**Options:**
- Story arc: problem → failed attempts → discovery → transformation
- Problem-agitation: dig deep into the pain, make them feel it
- Social proof stack: results, testimonials, numbers
- Educational: teach something valuable (reciprocity principle)
- Objection handling: address why they haven't acted yet

**Key insight**: Ads that address the NEGATIVE (objections, fears, failures) typically outperform purely positive ads.

### Bridge + CTA

**Only sell the next step:**
- Ad sells the CLICK, not the product
- Landing page sells the OPT-IN, not the purchase
- Booking page sells the APPOINTMENT, not the service

**CTA language matters:**
| Weak | Strong |
|------|--------|
| "Contact us" | "Answer a few questions to tailor your transformation" |
| "Sign up" | "See if you qualify" |
| "Buy now" | "Get yours before [scarcity]" |
| "Learn more" | "Discover how [specific result]" |

## Creative Types by Platform

### Meta/Facebook Ads

**Video (highest performance):**
- Talking head: founder/customer speaking to camera
- UGC: user-generated content, native feel
- Before/after: transformation visual
- Product demo: show it working
- Testimonial compilation: multiple results

**Production tips:**
- Subtitles always (85% watch without sound)
- Vertical for Stories/Reels, square for feed
- Native feel outperforms polished production
- Dynamic text overlays for retention
- Close-up shots > wide shots

**Image:**
- Product with lifestyle context
- Before/after comparison
- Stats/numbers as focal point
- Real faces (not stock photos)
- Text overlay under 20%

**Carousel:**
- Storytelling across cards
- Feature breakdown
- Social proof compilation
- Problem → solution flow

### Google Ads

**Responsive Search Ads (RSA):**
- 15 headlines (30 chars each)
- 4 descriptions (90 chars each)
- Pin important headlines to positions 1, 2, 3
- Use Dynamic Keyword Insertion (DKI): {KeyWord:Default Text}
- Use location insertion for local businesses
- Use ChatGPT to generate variations (with anti-AI-detection rules)

**Extensions/Assets (all applicable):**
- Sitelinks: 4 minimum
- Callouts: unique selling points
- Structured snippets: service/product categories
- Image extensions: relevant product photos
- Lead form extensions: for lead gen
- Price extensions: for transparent pricing
- Promotion extensions: for offers

## Schwartz's 5 Awareness Levels (Message Matching)

| Level | What they know | Your message | Example hook |
|-------|---------------|-------------|--------------|
| Unaware | Nothing about the problem | Talk about their world | "Most coaches spend 6 hours a day on Instagram..." |
| Problem-Aware | They have a problem | Agitate the problem | "Tired of posting every day with no leads?" |
| Solution-Aware | Solutions exist | Show your approach differs | "Forget posting 3x/day. Here's what actually works..." |
| Product-Aware | Know your product | Differentiate, prove | "Why 2,000+ coaches chose [Product] over agencies" |
| Most Aware | Ready to buy | Make the offer | "[Product] — 50% off this week only" |

## Angle Testing Framework

### What is an angle?
An angle is the specific perspective or hook you use to present the same product. One product can have 20+ angles.

### How to find angles:
1. List all customer "towards" motivations (desires)
2. List all "away from" motivations (pains/fears)
3. List previous failed attempts
4. For each: ask "why?" and "then what?" to go deeper
5. Each answer is a potential angle

### Testing methodology:
- Test 3-5 angles simultaneously
- Same audience, same budget, different creative
- Winner = best CTR + CR combination
- Create 3-5 variations of the winning angle
- Repeat

## UGC Creator Brief Template

```
PRODUCT: [name]
ANGLE: [specific hook/perspective]
TARGET AUDIENCE: [who]
DESIRED EMOTION: [what they should feel]

SCRIPT STRUCTURE:
1. Hook (0-3 sec): [exact opening line]
2. Problem (3-8 sec): [pain point to address]
3. Solution (8-20 sec): [how product solves it]
4. CTA (20-25 sec): [what to do next]

REQUIREMENTS:
- Film vertical (9:16)
- Natural lighting preferred
- Authentic, conversational tone
- Show product in use
- Include before/after if applicable
```
